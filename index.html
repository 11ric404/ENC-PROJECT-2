Introduction

Discord is a popular online communication application available on macOS, Windows, iOS, and Android devices. Discord is built around servers, channels, groups, text channels, as well as voice chats. As a mixed platform, it doesn't serve one sole purpose. Instead it joins video conferencing, group chats, and a modern form of community forums into one place, allowing for a wide range of usage contexts. While it initially had a gaming community focus, it is today used by teams, communities of shared hobbies, academic departments, and others around the world. If you've been asked to join Discord as part of your employment or academic commitments, you are in the right place.

In this tutorial, you will learn various core Discord features. That includes written communication, private communication, and real-time audio communication via text channels, direct messages, and voice channels, respectively. We will also address settings that enable you to stay organized and safe.

A Brief History of Discord

Within Discord's first year of release, it had already exploded to millions of users. First founded in 2015 by Jason Citron and Stan Vishnevskiy as a gaming-focused communication app, Discord saw success early. That success moved well-beyond gaming when it became widely used in schools, universities, as well to support remote workforces during the COVID-19 pandemic as an alternative to Zoom or Slack¹. Now in 2026 Discord reports over 500 million registered users, with wide uses from office hours in universities to corporate team coordination.

Who This Tutorial Is For

This tutorial is geared toward readers with no experience using Discord in the past. This intended reader will typically be an adult beginner who is downloading Discord as part of their academic or professional commitments. If you have used any communication platform such as an online email, Zoom, educational management software such as Canvas, or any other typical account-based online service, you are well equipped with all the technical knowledge needed to understand this tutorial.

What You Will Be Able to Do

By the end of this tutorial, you will be prepared to:

  • Join a server using an invite link
  • Create and set up a Discord account
  • Navigate the Discord desktop interface
  • Read and send messages in text channels
  • Join and participate in a voice channel
  • Send private direct messages to other users
  • Adjust your account settings for privacy and appearance
  • Manage your notification settings

Tools and Materials Needed

Before you begin, you'll need the following:

  • A desktop or laptop computer running Windows or macOS
  • A connection to the internet (WiFi and Ethernet both work)
  • A personal email address to register your account
  • A microphone and headset (optional, but recommended for voice communication)
  • A camera (optional, but recommended for video calls)

Good to Know

If you have used platforms like Microsoft Teams or Zoom in the past, Discord's voice channels will likely feel familiar. What makes Discord's voice channels unique is that they are always open. They require no specific meeting link or prior scheduling.

How to Use This Tutorial

This beginner-friendly tutorial builds on itself through 7 organized chapters discussing various vital areas of using Discord. If this is your first time with Discord, it is recommended to follow the chapters in order. You may also revisit a specific chapter to review a specific topic by utilizing the table of contents, designed as a navigation bar on the left-hand side of your screen. Clicking a topic name allows you to jump straight to it without scrolling the entire tutorial. Key Discord terms that are mentioned throughout this tutorial will be hyperlinked and are defined in the Glossary section toward the end of the website.
