Joining a Server

Learn how to join servers, how to get your bearings once you've joined one, how to find public servers, and what the purpose of a server is.

What Is a Server?

A server is essentially a community hub for a dedicated group of users. It is the main method of gathering in Discord. Your class, company department, club, or any kind of team can utilize its own Discord server. Every server has:

  • Channels: individual rooms for text or voice communication, typically separated by topic or purpose.
  • Members: Discord users who have joined the server. Located in the members panel as discussed in Chapter 2.
  • Roles: Group permission labels, assigned to users to allow or deny them from participating in different channels, making server level changes, or taking a variety of actions.
  • Rules: Universal, enforceable guidelines set by that server's administrator.

Most Discord users join an existing server rather than creating their own. You can join a server by acquiring a valid invite link specific to that server, or via the server discovery page.

Joining via Invite Link

In most contexts such as work or school, the server you need to join will not be publicly available on the discover page. You must obtain an invite link, commonly shared by a professor, manager, or classmate.

How to Join Using an Invite Link

1

Locate the invite link. Invite links are formatted like discord.gg/abc123 or a full URL like https://discord.gg/abc123.

2

Either click the link directly, or navigate to it by pasting it into your web browser.

3

If your link is valid, this site will contain a server preview. Review the server's name, as well as the additional information provided to ensure you were sent an invite to the correct server.

4

Click the green Accept Invite button.

Accepting an invite to join a Discord server.
5

Congratulations. You have joined your first server. The Discord app will open and the server you joined is now visible as an icon on your server selector panel.

6

Click the icon and verify you can view the server's channel list, chat area, as well as the member panel.

Finding a Public Server

Public servers are typically operated by and dedicated to official brands such as video game companies or famous musicians, in order to organize their communities of fans. Your team is unlikely to utilize a public server, but this is still good to know.

Using the Discover Page

1

Near the bottom of your server list, you may see a compass icon. Click it to navigate to the Discover page.

The Discover (compass) icon located near the bottom of the server list.
2

Use the labels at the top (Home, Gaming, Music, Entertainment, Science, Education) to filter your server search.

Filtering public servers by category on the Discover page.
3

If a server preview interests you, click anywhere on it to automatically join.

Viewing results and previews of available public servers.

Server Structure

First joining a server can be overwhelming. Understanding the server's structure allows you to get a solid grasp on how to use it.

Channel Categories

Servers utilizing common best practices will have channels grouped by categories resembling section headers. Some common, but not universal categories you may notice will be "ANNOUNCEMENTS," or "GENERAL." In class servers, a dedicated "OFFICE HOURS" section may also exist.

1

To open or close any of these collapsible categories, select the header.

A server's channel list organized into collapsible categories.

Pinned Messages and Welcome Information

Commonly, you will encounter channels like #welcome or #announcements. We recommend opening these channels first, since they contain relevant or critical information. Important information may also be pinned within specific channels.

1

To view messages that have been pinned, navigate to the top right of your screen.

2

Select the pin icon.

Locating the pinned messages icon in the top-right toolbar.

Reading the Rules Channel

Nearly every server will have unique rules regarding what kinds of activity are inappropriate in that community. Always locate those rules (typically in a #rules channel) and read them.

A typical rules channel providing important community guidelines.
⚠️
Important

In some servers, permission to participate in standard channels is restricted until users acknowledge the server's rules. If your server is set up that way, you may need to complete an initiation step, such as clicking to confirm. If you run into this, check the relevant channels for specific steps.
