Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

Running into an issue and don't know what to do? Here are the answers to some common problems new users run into.

Messaging and Channels

Why can't I type in certain channels? ▼

This is quite common, and typically intentional. It usually relates to one of the following:

  • Read-Only channels: These channels are not intended for conversation. They only allow messages from administrators, such as announcements.
  • Your assigned role does not have permission to message in that channel. DM a server administrator to request a role with permission.
  • Upon joining the first time, some servers require you to acknowledge the rules before messaging. Check for a rule channel and read the provided instructions for acknowledging the rules.
How do I find a server I've already joined? ▼

The server list sidebar on the far left contains every server you are in, represented by circular icons. Hover over those icons to reveal each server's name. Then click the icon to open it.

How do I leave a server I no longer need? ▼

Focus on the server sidebar. Find the intended server's circular icon, and right-click it. A context menu appears. Click "Leave Server." Click Leave Server again on the confirmation screen. The server is now gone from your server sidebar.

Audio and Voice

Why am I not hearing anyone in a voice channel? ▼

Work through these checks in order:

1

Locate the headphone icon in the user control bar on the bottom left. If the headphone has a red slash, you are deafened. Click it to undeafen.

2

Check your system volume. Ensure the volume on your PC or Laptop is set to an audible level.

3

Click the Discord Settings icon resembling a gear, click Voice & Video, and check your Output Device.

4

If you have multiple output devices, test each one until audio transmits via the expected device such as headphones or speakers.

5

Scroll down Voice & Video to find "Sounds." Click Preview Sound on each sound effect to test for audio output.

Why is my microphone not working? ▼

Try the following steps:

1

Locate the microphone icon in the user control bar on the bottom left. If the microphone has a red slash, you are muted. Click it to unmute.

2

Click the Settings icon, click Voice & Video, and check your Input Device.

3

If you have multiple input devices, test each one by clicking the "Mic Test" Button. A working microphone will produce a visual output showing your microphone's volume.

4

Enable microphone permissions for Discord:

  • On Windows, click your Windows key, open Settings, hit Privacy, and then pick Microphone. Locate the Discord app, ensuring it is enabled.
  • On macOS, open the Settings app in the toolbar, choose Privacy & Security, and select Microphone. Locate the Discord app, ensuring it is enabled.

Account and Privacy

Can other people see my email address or real name? ▼

No. Users can only see your chosen display name and username. Whether you want to use your real name as a display name is your choice and may be appropriate in academic or work settings.

Someone is acting inappropriately. What do I do? ▼

Take the following steps:

1

Block the user: Right-click their name, click Block. They will not be able to DM you or see your messages in servers.

2

Report the message: If their message breaks Discord Terms of Service, Right-click any message and select Report Message. A report menu opens. Follow the prompts to submit your report.

3

Notify your server administrator: If their message only breaks server rules, inform an administrator such as a professor or manager. They can remove or ban the user from the server.

I'm getting too many notifications. How do I reduce them? ▼

Find the server in your server selection panel. Right-click it. Hover over notifications and adjust your setting to Only @Mentions. You'll only be notified when you are mentioned in a message. For detailed steps, see Chapter 7: Managing Notifications.

Does Discord have a web app? ▼

Yes. Discord's web version has most of the features from the Desktop version, and most of the instructions in this tutorial will apply. However, the downloaded version is more suited for everyday use. Most individuals use the web version if they need to check their messages while using an unusual device, such as a hotel computer. Find the web version here: Discord.com/app.
