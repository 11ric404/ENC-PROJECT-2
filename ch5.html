Chapter 5

Direct Messages

Discord also enables you to have private conversations outside of servers. You'll now learn how to hold these DM (Direct Message) conversations.

What Is a Direct Message?

A Direct Message (DM) is Discord's equivalent to a private text message. These messages occur in private between two Discord users, or within a group chat, without ever using a server.

As you utilize Discord for your academic or professional role, you will likely utilize DMs for things like privately conversing with students, following up directly with a question you received in office hours or the workplace, or privately coordinating with colleagues.

Sending a DM to Another User

Ready to start using Direct Messages? There are two main ways to begin: from the Home screen or from within a server.

Method 1: From the Home Screen

1

Navigate to your home screen by clicking the Discord logo which will always be located at the top of the server selector panel.

The Discord logo used to navigate back to your Home screen.
2

To the right of the server selector panel, you will now see a Direct Messages Panel with a plus icon. Click the plus icon.

The plus (+) icon in the Direct Messages panel.
3

A search box titled "New Message" opens. Enter the username or display name of the Discord user you want to message.

Searching for a user to start a new private direct message.
4

Select the intended user from the list that appears. Click the blue "Create Message" button in the bottom right of the search box.

The "Create Message" button to initialize the private conversation.
5

A conversation window with the user is now visible. Type your message in the input bar and press Enter to send. Sending, Deleting, or reacting to messages can be done using the same buttons covered in Chapter 4.

Method 2: From Within a Server

1

If the members panel is not currently visible, select the Member List button, an icon resembling two human shapes in the top right of your screen.

The Member List button located in a server's top-right toolbar.
2

Scroll the server's members list until you locate the user you want to message.

3

Click their name or avatar. A small profile pop-up card will appear with the user's information. Ensure you have selected the correct account.

A user's profile pop-up card where you can view info and start a DM.
4

At the bottom of the pop-up card, a text box called "Message @<username>" is visible. Enter your message content and press enter.

The quick message box found at the bottom of any user's profile card.
5

A private DM window with that user will open, and your newly sent message is visible at the bottom of the conversation.

The DM Inbox

From your home screen, all your DM conversations are listed on the "Direct Messages" panel on the left of your screen. Each DM can be identified by the user's name and profile photo.

  • DMs are ordered by activity. Your most recent interactions are at the top.
  • To open any conversation, just click it.
  • Usernames in bold indicate an unread message.
  • To clean up a cluttered message list, hover over a DM and click the X that appears. This hides the conversation but does not delete the content.
The Direct Messages list showing your recent private conversations.

Creating a Group DM

Sometimes you need a private group conversation, but don't require a full server. This can be useful for small team coordination. Discord group DMs allow up to 10 users to communicate.

Starting a Group DM

1

On the Home screen, click the plus icon in the Direct Messages Panel.

The plus icon used to start a new individual or group message.
2

In the search box, find the first user you want to invite.

Searching for multiple users to invite to a new group DM.
3

Continue searching and selecting up to a total of 9 users.

Selecting multiple friends to include in the group conversation.
4

Optional: Enter a Group Name and Profile photo in the fields that appear.

Entering an optional group name and choosing a photo for the chat.
5

Click "Create Group Message" to start the group chat.

Clicking the "Create Group Message" button to start the private conversation.

Adding Someone to an Existing DM

1

Open an existing group chat or direct message.

2

In the top right of your screen, click the "Add friends to group DM" button which resembles two human figures behind a plus sign icon.

The "Add friends to group DM" icon in the top-right corner.
3

Search for and select the user(s) you want to add, then click Add.

Adding a new user to an existing group conversation.
ℹ️
Info: Group DM vs. Server

Group DMs are great for an informal conversation between a small group. Servers are a better fit for larger groups requiring organization, permission structures, and professionalism.

DM Etiquette and Privacy

Before you begin utilizing DMs in your academic or professional setting, here are some important details to consider:

  • DMs are private: Only the members of a DM can read the conversation, unlike in servers.
  • Privacy is not anonymity: Always DM respectfully and follow standard best practices for communication on any platform. In academic or professional settings, treat DMs like professional email.
  • Sometimes unwanted DMs like spam or scams may reach you. You can always block or report users (see Chapter 7: Privacy and Safety).
