Chapter 6

Using Voice Channels

You have likely encountered voice and video communication platforms like Zoom and Microsoft Teams. Discord's voice channels offer the same capabilities, minus the need for manually scheduling calls in advance. In this chapter you'll learn how to join voice channels and utilize them for office hours.

What Is a Voice Channel?

Voice channels are the alternative to text channels. They are a room inside a server which enables real-time audio communication between users. These rooms are available 24/7, can be accessed by any user with the appropriate role, and require zero scheduling or prior setup for each meeting.

Voice channels are identifiable on the channels panel by the speaker icon before their name, as opposed to the # symbol which identifies text channels.

A voice channel identified by the speaker icon in the server's channel list.

Voice Channels vs. Zoom

If you are used to Zoom, here is the key difference: a Zoom meeting requires multiple steps of setup. Discord's voice channels are more like conference rooms which are always open, making them ideal for drop-in style office hours.

Joining a Voice Channel

1

Ensure you are in the correct server. If a voice channel exists you can locate it in the channels list panel, identifiable by a speaker icon and channel name.

2

If users are in the voice channel, their usernames are visible under the voice channel name. Empty voice channels lack any usernames. Click the voice channel name to join it.

Clicking on a voice channel name to join the audio conversation.
3

An audible beep, as well as a "Voice Connected" label above the audio controls on the bottom of your screen confirm you've joined.

The "Voice Connected" status appearing above your user controls bar.
🎧
Tip: First Time Using Voice?

If you have not previously joined a voice channel, Discord may request permission to use your device's audio equipment. Click "allow."

Audio Controls in a Voice Channel

Your audio controls are managed via the user controls bar on the bottom left of your screen (See Chapter 2: The User Controls Bar).

Muting and Unmuting Yourself

  • Mute: Clicking the microphone icon in the user controls bar adds a red slash over the icon, indicating a successful mute.
The microphone icon without a slash when your audio is being transmitted.
  • Unmute: Click the microphone icon in the user controls bar. The red slash disappears, indicating a successful unmute.
The microphone icon showing a red slash when you are successfully muted.
🎤
Best Practice

Stay muted when not actively talking. Background noise like from a pet or a fan may distract other users.

Deafening and Undeafening

  • Deafen: Click the headphone icon in the user controls bar. A red slash appears over the headphone and microphone icons, indicating you can no longer hear others, and have been automatically muted.
  • Undeafen: Click the headphone icon again. Your audio will be restored.
The deafened state, which also automatically mutes your own microphone.

Use deafening when you want to step away from the voice chat briefly without fully disconnecting.

Adjusting the Volume of Other Participants

Sometimes, another user's audio can come across too loud or too quiet on your device. You can specifically adjust their volume without affecting other users.

1

Right click the user's name from the list of users in the voice channel.

2

An interaction menu including many options such as mute, block, and User Volume appears. Use the sliding scale to increase or decrease volume.

Adjusting the volume for a specific user through the right-click interaction menu.
ℹ️
Info: Local Volume Adjustment

User specific volume changes only apply to your own audio experience. No other users, including the subject of your volume change, will be alerted.

Sharing Your Screen

Another feature Discord shares with popular communication platforms is screen sharing. This will become useful if you ever need to present visual information or demonstrate something on your computer like code or math steps.

1

Join a voice channel.

2

In the user control bar, click the icon resembling a monitor with an arrow in it.

The "Share Your Screen" button located in the user controls section.
3

A dialog box asking you what you want to share appears. You can share a specific application window or share your entire screen.

The screen share dialog for choosing a specific window or your entire screen.
4

Select your preferred option and click Share Screen.

⚠️
Warning: Before You Share

Sharing your "entire screen" can cause unintended content to be shared such as notifications, other open apps or your desktop. Close anything you don't want visible to others.

Stopping Screen Share

1

Above the user control bar, a screen share control bar is visible. On the left of the control bar, find the end stream button resembling a monitor with an X icon in it.

The button used to end your current screen share session.
2

Click the end screen share button.

3

The screen share control bar disappears, confirming the screen share has ended.

Leaving a Voice Channel

1

Click the phone icon in the voice connection panel on the user control bar at the bottom-left of the screen.

The Disconnect button used to leave a voice channel.
2

An audible beep sound confirms you've been disconnected. Also notice that your name has been removed from the user list in the voice channel.
