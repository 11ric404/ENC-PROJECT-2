Chapter 7

Managing Notifications and Account Settings

Discord is well known for the comprehensive ability it offers to customize your notifications. You'll learn how to customize notifications at every level to meet your liking, as well as how to manage your account settings to personalize your appearance and protect your privacy and safety.

How Discord Notifications Work

Understanding notification tiers is the first step in preventing notification fatigue. Discord has three of them:

  • Server Tier: A broad setting that sets your default notification configuration for every channel in a server. A "All Messages" configuration ensures you're notified for every message in every channel of that server.
  • Channel Tier: Channel-Specific overrides to your server tier setting. You can mute a specific channel without muting the rest.
  • DM Tier: Direct Message notifications. All DMs typically generate a notification.
⚠️
Warning: Default Setting Alert

The default server tier notification setting is typically "All Messages." This could lead to dozens of notifications in a short time span in any busy server. Change this right after joining a server to prevent overload.

Changing Server Notification Settings

1

Find your server on the left-hand server selection panel. Right click the server.

2

Hover your mouse over Notification Settings.

The server notification settings menu.
3

Select one of the three options:

  • All Messages - Not recommended in busy servers
  • Only @Mentions - Mutes most messages unless you are specifically tagged by a user
  • Nothing – Never receive notifications from this server
Choosing your default notification preference for the server.
4

In academic or professional settings, selecting "Only @Mentions" provides the best balance, notifying you when you are addressed.

Suppressing @everyone and @here

As mentioned in Chapter 4, Good to Know: @Mention Etiquette, an @everyone mention notifies the entire server, and an @here mention notifies all online members of the server. When used frequently by administrators, this can be disruptive.

1

Once again, right click the server icon and hover over notification settings.

2

Select the checkbox beside "Suppress @everyone and @here."

Selecting the checkbox to suppress disruptive mass mentions.
3

You'll still be notified when you are mentioned specifically, but mass mentions will be muted.

Muting Individual Channels

You can also mute a specific channel without affecting how you receive notifications from other channels. You might use this to mute a busy chat channel while preserving notifications for an announcements channel.

1

Locate the channel in your channel list. Right click it.

Right-clicking a specific channel to access its context menu.
2

In the context window that opens, hover over Mute Channel.

Selecting the "Mute Channel" option from the context menu.
3

Select a mute duration: 15 minutes, 1 hour, 8 hours, 24 hours, or Until I turn it back on.

Choosing a duration for muting notifications from a specific channel.

Status Modes

Similar to platforms like Microsoft Teams, you can let others know whether you are available by adjusting your status. To change this:

1

Select your profile icon on the bottom left of your screen.

Clicking your profile icon to change your online status.
2

Hover over your status.

The various status mode options available: Online, Idle, DND, and Invisible.
3

Select a new status from the context menu that appears. You have the following choices:

  • Online: The default status when Discord opens. This shows others you are available.
  • Idle: Automatically set when Discord detects you have stepped away from your computer. This signals inactivity.
  • Do Not Disturb: A useful setting when you need to focus. This status limits pop-up notifications on your computer.
  • Invisible: Allows you to use Discord normally while presenting as offline to all other users.

Customizing Appearance

Dark Mode and Light Mode

Your theme determines what shade Discord appears in on your device.

1

Locate the settings icon resembling a gear in the bottom-left corner.

The User Settings gear icon located in the bottom-left corner.
2

Your settings menu opens, including a left sidebar to navigate between settings groups. Scroll down the sidebar and click Appearance.

Navigating to the "Appearance" section in the user settings sidebar.
3

Under theme, select your preference. Choose Light, Ash, Dark, or Onyx.

Selecting a theme preference like Light or Dark mode.

Text Size and Accessibility

Do you prefer larger text? You can adjust Discord's text size.

1

Locate the settings icon resembling a gear in the bottom-left corner.

2

Scroll down your left sidebar in settings until you locate "Accessibility."

Navigating to the "Accessibility" section in user settings.
3

Open the accessibility menu. The first setting "Text Readability" has a sliding bar, as well as a preview of what text will look like. Click and hold the sliding bar. Drag it until the text in the preview window matches your preferred size.

Adjusting text readability with the font size sliding bar.

The accessibility menu also offers a variety of more niche features to customize your experience, including adjusting space between messages, zoom level to adjust panel sizing, and a toggle to disable animations.

Privacy and Safety Settings

Discord has several settings that protect your privacy. You should review these when first setting up your account.

Restricting Who Can Send You DMs

1

Locate the settings icon resembling a gear in the bottom-left corner.

Opening the User Settings menu from the bottom-left gear icon.
2

On your left sidebar, click "Content and Social."

Selecting "Content and Social" in the settings sidebar to manage privacy.
3

Scroll down and locate "Direct Message Spam." Select one of three options to determine how DMs sent to you are filtered:

  • Filter All: All DMs to you from friends and non-friends are filtered for Spam.
  • Filter from non-friends (default): DMs from friends are always allowed. New DMs from non-friends are filtered for spam.
  • Do not filter: All DMs reach you.
Filtering Direct Message spam in your privacy and safety settings.
4

Below Direct Message Spam, locate "Social Permissions." This provides you with two options for how DMs from members you share servers with are treated.

5

In Social Permissions, click the dropdown arrow and select whether you want to adjust settings for All Servers you are in, or a specific server.

Selecting a specific server to adjust social permissions for its members.
6

Toggle on or off these two options:

  • Direct Messages: Turning this on allows DMs from users of the selected server to reach you.
  • Message Requests: Turning this on filters messages from users Discord believes you do not know.
Toggling direct message and message request permissions for server members.
💡
Tip: Finding Filtered Messages

Sometimes an important message gets filtered. You can review filtered messages from the "Message Requests" section in your direct messages panel. To navigate to your direct messages panel, see Chapter 5: Direct Messages.

Blocking a User

1

Locate the user anywhere in Discord. This can be done from your direct messages if you've interacted before, from the members panel of a server or group chat, or from any message sent by the user.

Opening the context menu by right-clicking a username.
2

Right click the user to open a context menu.

Selecting the red "Block" option in the user's context menu.
3

Select the red "Block" button. The user will no longer see your profile or messages in groups or shared servers.

ℹ️
Note: Blocks Only Apply to You

If a user you've blocked is active in a server or group, you'll still see messages from other users responding to them. If a user is seemingly talking to themselves, they are likely conversing with a user you've blocked.

Reporting a User

1

Like blocking, you must right-click on the user's profile from anywhere in Discord.

Right-clicking a user's profile to open the context menu for reporting.
2

From the context menu, select "profile."

Viewing a user's full profile before proceeding with a report.
3

Select the "…" button below their username to open a context window.

4

Select "Report User Profile." This feature lets you anonymously submit context to Discord for why you believe the user has broken Discord's terms of service.

👤
Tip: Notify Your Administrator

If you notice a user breaking a server-specific rule rather than Discord TOS or the Law, you should notify a server administrator rather than reporting the user. An admin, such as a professor or a manager, can kick out or ban the user from the server.
