Chapter 1

Creating Your Account

Before you get started with Discord, here is how to download the Discord application, make an account, verify that account via email, and set up your profile.

Downloading Discord

The ideal way to access Discord is using the desktop application, available on both Windows and macOS. Discord can also be used via a web browser as well as on various types of mobile devices, but for your academic or professional needs, the desktop app is recommended.

📱
Note on Mobile

Discord has a popular mobile app for iOS and Android, but this tutorial focuses on the downloadable desktop application. This interface on mobile devices is slightly different, and will not be covered in this tutorial.

Downloading on Windows

1

Navigate to Discord.com on any web browser you're comfortable with.

The Discord.com homepage.
2

Near the bottom of the page you will see a "Download for Windows" button. Click it. The installer file (DiscordSetup.exe) will begin downloading.

3

Open your device's Downloads folder and double-click DiscordSetup.exe to run the installer.

Locating the DiscordSetup.exe installer in the Windows Downloads folder.
4

Discord will install automatically. When the installation is complete, the Discord login screen will open.

Downloading on macOS

1

Navigate to Discord.com on any web browser you're comfortable with.

2

Near the bottom of the page you will see a "Download for Mac" button. Click it. A .dmg file will download to your Downloads folder.

The Discord.com homepage for macOS users.
3

Open the .dmg file and drag the Discord icon into your Applications folder.

Dragging the Discord icon into the Applications folder on macOS.
4

Open Discord from your Applications folder or Launchpad. After loading up, you should see the login screen.

After Installation

When Discord opens for the first time, you can expect up to 2 minutes of loading depending on your device's speed. After that, you will see a login screen allowing you to either log in to your account or register one. Since you do not have an account yet, you will click Register in the next section.

Creating a New Account

Since you haven't signed up for a Discord account in the past, you need to create one now. The process takes about two minutes.

ℹ️
Note

If your organization has already created an account for you, you may skip this step and log in with your existing credentials.

1

On the login screen, click Register.

The Discord login screen where you can register a new account.
2

Fill out the registration form with the following information:

Email: IMPORTANT: Use a permanent email address you think you will always have constant access to. For example, a student address provided by your University may not be a good option as you may lose access after Graduating. This email is used for verification and account recovery.

Display Name: Feel free to use anything you'd like, but because this is your visible name shown to other users, ensure it is in line with your organization's standards. You may also change this later.

Username: Your unique account identifier. This may take a few iterations if your initial preferred username is taken by an existing account.

Password: Your password must be a minimum of 8 characters. For security reasons, Discord will reject passwords without enough complexity.

Date of Birth: Enter your date of birth. Discord's terms of service restrict account sign-ups to those 13 years and older.

Filling out the account registration form.
3

Check the box to confirm you are not a robot, then click Continue.

4

Discord will prompt you to complete a CAPTCHA verification. These typically consist of a puzzle or typing challenge. Follow the on-screen instructions.

🔐
Password Tip

Discord accounts are known to be targeted by malicious actors. It is recommended to use a unique password you have not previously used with another website.

Verifying Your Email

1

Upon registering your account, check your email inbox for a verification email from Discord.

2

Look for an email from Discord with the subject "Verify Your Email Address." If it does not arrive within a few minutes, click the resend button on Discord. Additionally, check your spam folder for this email.

An email from Discord with the verify email button highlighted.
3

Click the Verify Email button inside the email.

4

Your browser will open and confirm that your email has been verified. Use your new credentials to log in to Discord.

⚠️
Important

Discord may allow you to utilize some features without verifying your email, but the majority of server features will be restricted. Complete this step before moving on.

Setting Up Your Profile

You're almost ready to join servers. Before doing so, you should make your profile look professional. If you use Discord for work or in academia, making your display name and profile photo recognizable can help colleagues or students identify you easily.

Uploading a Profile Picture

1

In the bottom left of your Discord window, you will notice a gear icon. This is your user settings menu. Click it.

Locating the User Settings gear icon in the bottom-left corner.
2

In the left panel of Settings, click My Account.

Selecting "My Account" in the user settings sidebar.
3

You should now see security information pertaining to your account. Click the blue "Edit User Profile" button near the top left.

The "Edit User Profile" button in the My Account section.
4

This is a customization menu for your profile. Near the top of the menu, click the blue "Change Avatar" button.

Choosing the "Change Avatar" button to upload a photo.
5

Select an image from your computer. Discord accepts JPG, PNG, and GIF formats. To ensure your photo displays properly, the recommended size is: 128×128 pixels or larger.

6

Crop the image if prompted, then click Apply.

Setting Your Display Name

Your Discord Profile has two types of names. It is important to understand the difference:

Username: An account identifier unique to you. This is typically not displayed publicly, but is visible if a user inspects your profile. This is what you'd share when having someone add you as a friend. Username changes are limited.

Display Name: This is your publicly visible name within groups, private messages, or servers. You may change this whenever you'd like, and even adjust your name per server.

1

Select the My Account tab and choose Edit User Profile.

The "Edit User Profile" button in the My Account section.
2

Click the Display Name field. Enter your desired name and click Save Changes.

The Display Name field where you can enter your preferred name.
🎓
Academic Best Practice

In educational settings, you likely want instructors or students to recognize your profile in text or voice channels. We recommend using your real first and last name as your display name.

Adding an Optional Bio

If you scroll down from your display name, you also have the choice to add a bio. Here you may want to list things like your department, role, or in academic settings, the course number. This setting isn't required, but provides further help identifying you.
