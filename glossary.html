Glossary of Key Terms

Are you lost in the jargon? Use this glossary to find definitions for any Discord terminology used in this tutorial.

ℹ️
How to Use This Glossary

Terms are listed alphabetically. If one of these terms is used and you are unfamiliar with it, click it to directly locate its definition here. To return to where you were, click your browser's back button.

Bot
Useful automated accounts that can perform many tasks from sending reminders, welcoming new members, to moderating text channels for spam and foul language via automated language filtering. Bots can only be added by administrators.
Channel
A specific room within a Discord server used for communication. Text channels (marked with #) are for written messages and file sharing. Voice channels (marked with a speaker icon) are for live audio conversations. Channels are organized into categories within a server.
Deafen
This mutes audio transmission from all other users. It also automatically mutes your own microphone. This is useful for briefly stepping away from a conversation without leaving a voice channel.
Direct Message (DM)
A private conversation between two Discord users, or a small group of up to 10 users, that takes place outside of any server.
Invite Link
The main method for granting users access to a Discord server. Invite links are a URL formatted as discord.gg/unique_code and pertain to a specific server. These are shareable across the web, email, or anywhere else, and when clicked, allow a user to join that server. They can be set to expire after a certain period or usage count.
@Mention
A tag/notification method to alert a specific user. When a user's username or display name is placed directly after an @ such as in @JohnDoe, that user will receive a notification for that message (unless they have adjusted their notification settings to prevent them).
Mute
A method for disabling your own microphone audio, indicated by a red slash over your microphone icon in your user control bar. You can still hear others.
Ping
An informal term sometimes used interchangeably with a "mention," "alert," or "notification." Pinging someone means you have sent them a DM, used @Mention to tag them in a message, or used any other method to get their attention.
Role
A label representing a permission group in a server. Roles are assigned by administrators or moderators and allow or prohibit members from performing a wide array of actions. In an academic setting, a "Professor" role might allow for sending announcements, editing announcements, or deleting the announcements channel altogether, while a "student" role might only allow users to read them.
Server
An organized community hub for a dedicated group of users. It is the main method of gathering in Discord. Your class, company department, club, or any kind of team can utilize its own Discord server. Users can join multiple different servers.
Voice Channel
The alternative to text channels. A room inside a server which enables real-time audio communication between users. These rooms are available 24/7, can be accessed by any user with the appropriate role, and require zero scheduling or prior setup for each meeting. The ideal choice for drop-in office hours.

Voice channels are identifiable on the channels panel by the speaker icon before their name. Voice channels also have features like a Zoom call, such as sharing your screen.
